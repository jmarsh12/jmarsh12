Fall

Autumn, also known as fall in the United States, is one of the four temperate seasons. Outside the tropics, autumn marks the transition from summer to winter, in September or March depending on your hemisphere. Autumn is the season when the duration of daylight becomes noticeably shorter and the temperature cools considerably. Day length decreases and night length increases as the season progresses until the Winter Solstice in December and June depending on your hemisphere. One of its main features in temperate climates is the striking change in color for the leaves of deciduous trees as they prepare to shed.
